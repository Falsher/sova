Veneers

Veneers - it is an orthopedic construction, which is a thin light plate that covers the outer surface of the teeth. They are a great help to solve the problem of darkened teeth for various reasons, which can not be corrected by whitening, adjust the shape and size of the teeth or remove the gap between the teeth.

They are a great help to solve the problem of darkened teeth for various reasons, which can not be corrected by whitening, adjust the shape and size of the teeth or remove the gap between the teeth.

What are veneers?

It is customary to distinguish between two types of veneers depending on the material and the way in which they are made:

  • therapeutic veneers from composite materials
  • orthopedic veneers from ceramics
The installation of therapeutic veneers is also known as dental restoration. Veneers from a composite similar to that used to make fillings are formed in layers directly on the patient's teeth, and then sanded and polished. Ceramic veneers are created by dental technicians in the laboratory of the finest ceramics, their thickness does not exceed 0.5-1 mm. Despite the fact that they are more difficult to manufacture than composite, you will need only 2-3 visits to the clinic. And reliability and aesthetics of veneers from ceramics are unsurpassed.

Ceramic veneers VS composite veneers.

In this comparison, ceramic veneers unconditionally win. No matter how high-quality the composite is, it does not cease to be plastic, and plastic tends to flake off over time, fade and change color. Ceramic veneers have the advantage that the material from which they are made, is not susceptible to pigmentation and perfectly retains both color and shape, as well as completely transmits color and transmits the natural luster of living teeth. In addition, ceramic veneers are made exclusively in the dental laboratory and fit perfectly to the tooth surface, thus minimizing the risk of secondary caries between the pad and the tooth.
