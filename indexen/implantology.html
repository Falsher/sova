Implantology

What is a tooth implant (implant)? An implant is an artificial tooth root made of biocompatible titanium material. Dental implants, in fact, restore the missing roots of decayed and extracted teeth and allow you to perform fixed or conditionally removable prosthetics with crowns or bridges. The number of implants that need to be installed depends on how many teeth have been lost.

Advantages of implantation over prosthetics:

  • The titanium implant is implanted into the gum bone and perfectly fuses with it (takes root). The result is a reliable, non-removable design.
  • Titanium implants are very reliable and durable. Subject to the rules of hygiene and planned visits to the dentist, implants are given a lifetime warranty.
  • It is possible to restore both one lost tooth and several. At the same time, adjacent healthy teeth are not grinded.
  • Stopping bone loss at the site of the missing tooth. Without functional load, the bone atrophies very quickly.

The main condition for implantation is the presence of a sufficient amount of bone. The height, width of the alveolar process, topography of the neurovascular bundle and maxillary sinuses are taken into account. In case of insufficient amount of bone, open or closed sinus lifting is performed (Kharkov). Our dental implantologist must take into account all these points. So, we talked about the qualifications of dentists, about what dental implantation is and about the advantages of implantation over prosthetics. Now let's get acquainted with the types of implantation: what are they and how are they different?

One-stage implantation in Kharkov

During the initial surgery, the implant and the healing abutment are installed. This is followed by the engraftment of the implant, and 3-6 months after the final healing, the abutments are installed and permanent prosthetics are performed.

Implantation with simultaneous loading

Under favorable conditions, the implantation of the tooth can be carried out immediately after the removal of the decayed tooth or root. The temporary plastic crown will be installed immediately. In fact, the patient leaves the doctor with a new tooth. However, there are certain conditions for such express implantation:

  • inability to cure a tooth therapeutically and to have a prosthesis
  • the height of the alveolar process at the site of the proposed implantation is at least 7-10 mm, sufficient width and bone density
  • lack of an active inflammatory process in the bone
  • satisfactory hygiene and complete sanitation of the oral cavity
  • absence of somatic pathology

Two-stage implantation in the dental office

It involves the installation of implants and suturing of the mucous membrane above them. After 4-6 months, the second surgical stage is performed - the installation of a gum former. After healing, abutment placement and permanent prosthetics. Abutment – it is the connecting element of the structure. On the one hand, it is screwed into the root implant, and its protruding part is the prototype of the tooth stump, on which the crown or bridge is fixed. There are standard and custom abutments. Customized ones are often made from zirconium oxide. In combination with a metal-free crown made of the same material, an ideal aesthetic effect is achieved. These teeth often look prettier than their own. It should be remembered only that the level of hygiene in the presence of implants must also be ideal!
